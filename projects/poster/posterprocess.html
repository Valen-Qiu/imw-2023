Poster Process

2 Week - Solo Work

1 - Research

"What comes after the environment?" is a lecture held in the school. I started looking for various designs and formats because of the nature of this occasion. The primary objective of this poster was to convey essential details about the lecture and pique the audience's interest.

As I delved into my research online, I gained a more profound insight into the event, prompting me to refine the information. Crafting the information hierarchy proved to be a challenging aspect of poster design.

2 - Prototype & Test

Considering that this is a page design, it may be presented on both the web and mobile phones. I decided to try both vertical and horizontal layouts.

For the theme color of the poster, I chose several hues associated with the "environment" to create a cohesive visual identity. To convey varying levels of information effectively, I employed a combination of text sizes, weights, and styles.

I checked the final product on my phone and PC after finishing the first draft. Based on comments from various friends regarding the difficulties in finding information, the layout was further modified.

3 - Coding & Animation

I separated the poster into pieces first using the technique taught in class. In contrast to the static poster, I began to consider which elements could benefit from animation to increase interaction and attention. I'm trying to add inconspicuous animation effects to the title and some graphics in order to show the theme "after" through animation.

4 - Reflection

In this project, I found that I could easily utilize HTML and CSS to accomplish the design. One of the key challenges I encountered was selecting the most fitting animation effects to ensure that the visually appealing motion design enhances the overall design's effectiveness. This will undoubtedly be a path I'll continue to explore in future projects..
