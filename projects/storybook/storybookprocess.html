Stroybook Process

1 Week - Solo Work

1 - Research & Ideation

I do not have any coding experience. My primary goal was to acquaint myself with HTML and CSS while exploring various methods of illustrating images. Initially, I scoured numerous three-page comic stories as potential project ideas. However, I soon realized that these stories demanded extensive graphic design skills and intricate scripting. Therefore, I pivoted from traditional storytelling to a more interactive approach, where I would depict processes such as crafting sushi, tidying a room, or nurturing a tree. Users could actively engage with these narratives by clicking buttons. Ultimately, I settled on the theme of "Creating Onigiri," akin to a classic restaurant management game, offers users the freedom to make flexible choices and interact throughout the experience.

Due to the project's specific requirements and limited timeframe, I was unable to demonstrate complex graphic effects in HTML and CSS. Consequently, I searched for simple illustrations as references to enhance my approach to depicting various items, including foods and background. This approach helped me brainstorm more effective ways to represent elements by using geometric shapes.

2 - Low-fi & Test

I employed a low-fi approach to define the elements on each page and determine the overall story's length. Initially, I attempted to gamify the storybook by including instructions on how to play on the homepage. However, I quickly recognized that this "game" was overly simplistic, and users could have a fulfilling experience without requiring an onboarding process.

Additionally, I designed a collection of UI elements and food graphics to assist me in achieving a more polished, high-fi representation of the project.

3 - Hi-fi & Test

During the high-fidelity design phase, I adjusted the positioning, color, and sizing of elements to create a visually pleasing and comfortable page layout. However, when I prototyped the user flow, I realized that the flexible combination of four foods could potentially result in dozens of results. I could not achieve this easily through HTML and CSS. Therefore, I made adjustments to the page layout and user flow, transitioning from the selection of multiple foods to a simplified approach of asking users, "Do you want to choose this food?"

After testing the prototype in Figma, I found that the storybook lacked some "surprise". Therefore, I made adjustments to the final page. In the event that the user selects all the foods, it will result in a failure, and prompt the user to restart the storybook. This addition aims to introduce an element of challenge and unpredictability, enhancing the overall engagement of users.

4 - Coding

I expanded my understanding of HTML and CSS by watching numerous YouTube video tutorials. These tutorials provided me with valuable insights, especially when dealing with complex graphics. However, some design details had to be modified due to implementation constraints within the code. Moreover, a significant portion of my time was devoted to ensuring that each element within the storybook could seamlessly adapt to page changes. This responsive design approach aimed to enhance the overall user experience and maintain consistency throughout the project.

5 - Reflection

In just three days, I went from knowing nothing to completing this project. While there are still many details that could be improved, I take immense pride in what I've achieved. This project has been a tremendous learning experience, and I've gained two invaluable takeaways from it.

1. Describe what I want in one sentence

When I search for tutorials, I sometimes struggle to articulate precisely the effect I'm trying to achieve, which can lead to spending extra time searching for the right solution. However, this experience has underscored the vital role of clear communication, especially when working with engineers as a UI/UX designer in the future. Being able to express my design intentions and requirements accurately will undoubtedly be a valuable skill to facilitate collaboration and streamline the development process.

2. Plan and modularize before code

In coding, achieving the same result can often be accomplished through various methods. I attempted to simplify my coding process, but I the desired effect was not achieved optimally. Consequently, upon reviewing my code, you may notice that it lacks cleanliness. However, this experience has reinforced me to plan before coding to reduce development time in the future.
