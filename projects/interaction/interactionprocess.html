Interaction Process

5 Week - Solo Work

1 - Research

The word I was assigned was “hover,” a commonly employed technique in web design known for its ability to boost user engagement. I started with gather various manifestations of hover, such as contextual hover cards, adaptive contextual menus, dynamic visual feedback, and gamified interactions.

The incorporation of hover in these interactive websites generates distinctive visual effects and user experiences, serving as a rich source of inspiration for me.

2 - Brainstorm & Prototype1

The central concept behind "Hover" is that the page undergoes visual changes when the mouse is in motion. I aim to develop an interactive website for my design studio with this feature.

Having conceived the foundational elements of the page, I delineated the typography and information hierarchy using a low-fidelity approach. Informed by research, I contemplated which elements could effectively convey information through hover interactions.

The first involves a clickable button, undergoing variations like color changes or underlining. The second pertains to the initial interface upon entering the webpage. I aspire to imprint the idea of "design for well-being and the future" on users. Consequently, I experimented with different effects on the main image, incorporating elements such as "focus" and "parallax." Additionally, for showcasing case studies, I explored various methods gleaned from YouTube tutorials, including elaborate picture animation effects during hovering and loading effects for information. However, in order to maintain the overall simplicity of the webpage and optimize the visual impact of the images, I ultimately opted for the current approach involving changes in brightness and size.

3 - Improvement & Prototype2

Following a more in-depth conversation with Peter, I discovered that the primary emphasis of this project lies not in the presentation of information on the website but in infusing interactive elements with a playful, game-like nature. Consequently, I embarked on a reconsideration of how to incorporate engaging hover effects into a display-oriented website and strike a harmonious balance between visual aesthetics, information, and interactivity

I gave up adding too many animation interactive elements at the beginning of the page because I wanted the user’s attention to remain on the portfolio and the website itself. I decided to add a fun background animation interaction at the end.

Utilizing my knowledge of visual effects in p5.js, I experimented with effects such as "flow field," "star-shutting sky," and a "particle system"

However, these visual presentations seemed incongruent with the overall theme of the website, appearing overly intricate. Subsequently, I reevaluated my approach and employed animated color changes. This not only facilitated seamless visual transitions but also introduced an element of playfulness for users

4 - Coding

I referred to many tutorials. Initially, I focused on resolving the visual effects associated with color blur and fusion. Subsequently, I delved into the creation of original animations. The iterative process involved fine-tuning numerous parameters until a satisfying effect emerged.

The evolution of the mouse's behavior prompted me to contemplate incorporating an engaging animated rotation for the cursor in other information areas. This change not only introduced an element of whimsy but also provided an opportunity to refine the hover effect of the buttons, further enhancing the overall interactive experience.
