Index Process

12 Week - Solo Work

1 - Research & Brainstorm

I aimed to explore more creative and engaging interactive approaches. After in-depth research on various websites and exploring a series of interesting JavaScript libraries, I ultimately decided to make cards the central theme of the index project. Cards not only possess unique randomness and diversity but also offer users a novel and engaging experience. The choice of this theme is intended to break the boundaries of traditional design, providing users with a more unique and enjoyable visual experience.

Initially, I experimented with designing different styles of pages to achieve more creative expression, such as a hand-drawn sketchbook style and a scrolling effect gallery. However, through extensive discussions with Peter, I gradually realized the critical importance of maintaining page consistency for the overall user experience. Consequently, I adjusted the design direction, focusing on the card theme to ensure overall design consistency and coherence.

2 - Prototype1

Due to my limited experience in web design, I referred to the layout of some HTML and CSS templates. The overall design style was determined through wireframes. However, during the actual coding process, I encountered a new challenge due to the varying content and information on each card. To overcome this obstacle, I made multiple adjustments, reorganizing the layout of the cards. Especially concerning the browsing experience on mobile devices, I decided to adopt a strategy of displaying only one column of cards to ensure responsiveness and user-friendliness, allowing users to comfortably browse content on different devices.

3 - Prototype2 & Coding

To ensure coordination between interactive elements and image elements, I extensively used Figma for design and created a series of futuristic icons. Considering the holistic user experience, particularly when each page contains a substantial amount of information, I introduced key interactive design elements. In the bottom right corner of the page, I added a "back to top" function for users to quickly return to the beginning of the page. Additionally, to ensure users could easily access the navigation menu at any time, I fixed the menu bar at the top of the page, enabling users to navigate to different sections effortlessly. These design decisions aim to enhance the smoothness and convenience of the user experience, making the overall interaction more natural and intuitive.

At the project's inception, I considered using three.js to build a 3D visual effect website. However, due to time constraints, I abandoned this idea. Instead, I discovered the Tilt JS library, which allowed me to create a 3D visual effect in the simplest way possible. Therefore, I decided to add this effect to each card.

Additionally, in project3, I experimented with an animated background. I believe this is a non-intrusive interactive approach that provides users with some freedom to interact. For the background, I used effects from the tsParticles library, adding a style suitable for web pages.
